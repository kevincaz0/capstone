Neoclassicism

After the Renaissance was seen as the climax of art, there was a halt in advancements and many were making use of all the previous discoveries without thinking to continue creating all throughout the 18th and 19th century. However, with the intent to bring back classicism came the introduction to neo-classic architecture that included, Romanesque revival, Gothic revival and Federal Style Architecture which can also be called American Neo-Classicism. Neoclassism is a relatively simplified form of architecture that consists of blank walls, the simplicity of geometic forms and an impressive use of columns. Details were those that originated from Doric Greek and Roman styles. Buildings were made out of stone, brick and marble. Overall, this style put an even greater emphasis on symmetry and simplicity in large structure.
